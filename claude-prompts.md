# 5 Claude Prompts for Building Your First Digital Product
**The First Offer Kit — Cvetanichin**

---

Use these prompts in order. Copy and paste each one directly into Claude. Fill in the [brackets] with your specific details before sending.

---

## Prompt 1 — Outline

Use this to generate the full structure of your product before writing anything.

```
I am creating a digital product for [describe your buyer in one sentence].

My offer: [paste your offer statement from the worksheet]

The product delivers this one result: [your three buyer outcomes]

The format is: [PDF guide / mini toolkit / template pack]

Please create a detailed outline for this product. Include:
- An introduction section
- 3–5 main sections, each with a clear teaching goal
- A brief description of what each section covers
- One concrete exercise or output per section (something the buyer fills in or creates)
- A short closing section

Tone: calm, practical, premium, non-hype. Written for intelligent adult women who are skeptical of online income claims and want something credible and immediately useful. No motivational language. No hollow affirmations.
```

---

## Prompt 2 — Draft One Section

Use this once per section. Do not ask Claude to draft the full product at once.

```
I am writing Section [number]: [section title] of my digital product.

The goal of this section is: [what the buyer should be able to do or decide after reading it]

The output for this section is: [the one thing she will write, complete, or produce]

Please draft this section. Length: 500–700 words. 

Tone: calm, warm, practical. Write as if you are a knowledgeable friend who has done this before and is explaining it clearly — not lecturing, not hyping. No filler phrases. No "in today's world" or "now more than ever." No bullet points unless they genuinely aid clarity. Real paragraphs, real sentences.

Start with the most important idea, not with a preamble about what this section will cover.
```

---

## Prompt 3 — Refine and Tighten

Use this after you have edited a draft section and want a final pass before including it.

```
Please review and tighten the following text. 

Goals:
- Remove any filler words or phrases that do not add meaning
- Improve sentence flow where it feels choppy or unnatural
- Ensure each paragraph makes one clear point
- Remove anything that sounds like generic AI writing (hollow phrases, vague affirmations, unnecessary hedging)
- Preserve the original tone — calm, warm, practical, non-hype

Do not change the core meaning or add new ideas. Only refine what is already there.

[Paste your edited section here]
```

---

## Prompt 4 — Generate Title Options

Use this after your product is drafted to find the right name.

```
My digital product helps [your buyer] do [what] so they can [result].

Please generate 10 possible product title options. 

Rules:
- Each title should be clear and specific — the buyer should understand what it is without explanation
- Avoid clever wordplay if it sacrifices clarity
- Avoid generic "make money online" language
- The tone should feel premium, calm, and trustworthy — not flashy
- Include a mix of styles: some descriptive, some outcome-focused, some phrase-based

After listing the 10 titles, tell me which 2 you think are strongest and why.
```

## Prompt 5 — Sales Page Copy

Use this after your product is complete and you are ready to write the sales page.

```
I need a sales page for my digital product. Here are the details:

Product name: [title]
Who it's for: [buyer description]
The problem it solves: [describe her frustration and situation]
What's inside: [list sections or key components]
The result she gets: [your three buyer outcomes]
Price: €[price]
Format: [PDF / PDF + Notion / etc.]
Delivery: instant download

Please write a complete sales page using this structure:
1. Hook — 1–2 sentences that name her exact problem
2. Problem — 2–3 sentences in her words: what she's tried, what hasn't worked
3. Promise — what she will have after using this product
4. What's Inside — a bullet list of 5–8 specific things inside the product
5. This is for you if — 3 bullets describing the exact buyer
6. Price and CTA — state the price, one clear button label, one line of reassurance

Tone: honest, calm, premium. No hype, no false urgency, no countdown timers, no "this will change your life." Write for a skeptical, intelligent woman who has seen too many online income promises and needs something that sounds real.

Length: 350–500 words total.
```
